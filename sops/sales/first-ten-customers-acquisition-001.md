# First 10 Customers Acquisition Framework

## Metadata
- **Source**: Jen Abel - Lenny's Podcast (Episodes 1 & 2)
- **Domain**: Sales / Founder-Led Sales
- **Type**: Process
- **Applicable To**: Pre-seed and seed stage founders, early-stage startups (0-500K ARR)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner to Intermediate

## Overview
A systematic approach to acquiring your first 10 customers through manual, high-touch outreach and learning-first conversations. This framework prioritizes validation and learning over revenue, helping founders discover product-market fit while building initial customer relationships.

## When to Use
- You're a pre-seed or seed stage founder with no customers yet
- You have an MVP or early product but haven't validated the market
- You're transitioning from idea to first revenue
- You need to learn what resonates with your target market
- You're before you hire your first salesperson (typically before $500K-1M ARR)

## Prerequisites
- A clear hypothesis about who your target customer might be
- Some form of product or service offering (even if incomplete)
- A novel insight or unique perspective on a problem
- Willingness to spend 15-20 minutes per prospect on outreach
- Founder availability to take sales calls personally

## Procedure

### Step 1: Identify Your First 30 Prospects Manually

**Goal**: Create a focused list of ideal prospects without using automation tools.

- **Find 30 specific people** you want to learn from and potentially sell to
- Ask yourself: "Can I even find these people easily?" (If no, your targeting may be too narrow)
- Look for commonalities across the 30:
  - Size of team they work on
  - Industry they're in
  - Length of time in their role
  - Previous roles in their career
  - Company stage or maturity
- **Don't use tools yet** - this is manual discovery to understand your market
- Write down parameters you notice (these become your targeting criteria later)

**Red Flags**:
- If you can't easily find 30 people, your market may be too small or poorly defined
- If there are zero commonalities, you haven't narrowed your ICP enough

### Step 2: Craft Personalized Outreach Messages

**Goal**: Write compelling, concise messages that get responses through relevance and intrigue.

For each of the 30 prospects, spend 15-20 minutes crafting a message with these four elements:

1. **Relevance** (Why this person, why now)
   - Reference their role, recent funding, or current focus
   - Example: "I noticed you recently raised a Series A and are targeting enterprise customers..."

2. **Counterintuitive Insight** (The hook that makes them pause)
   - Share something surprising or different
   - Use "shock value" or challenge conventional thinking
   - Example: "Zero to one sales talent doesn't exist. That's why we built this model."
   - **Avoid**: "We're better than X" - focus on different, not better

3. **Problem Focus** (Not solution)
   - Talk about the problem you want to solve
   - Don't mention your product or solution yet
   - Leave them wanting more

4. **Concise Format** (No scrolling on mobile)
   - 3-4 sentences maximum
   - Must fit on a mobile screen without scrolling
   - Use short paragraphs

**Example Template**:
```
[Name],

[Relevance: 1 sentence about why you're reaching out to them specifically]

[Counterintuitive insight: 1-2 sentences with your unique perspective on the problem]

[Ask: Would you be open to a 15-minute conversation about this?]

[Your name]
```

**Test Your Message**:
- Use Gmail's "replay email" feature to hear how it sounds
- Would YOU respond to this message?
- Does it sound passive-aggressive when read aloud?

### Step 3: Multi-Channel Outreach

**Goal**: Reach prospects through multiple touchpoints without being spammy.

- **Email**: Send your crafted message to their work email
- **LinkedIn DM**: Send a similar (but not identical) message
- **Cold Call**: Yes, actually call them - interest rates are higher than email
- **Twitter DM**: If they're active on Twitter, try this channel

**Important Notes**:
- Don't blast all channels at once - space them out over a few days
- Cold calling has surprisingly high interest rates for founder-led outreach
- The founder's name/title carries different weight than a salesperson's

### Step 4: Track and Learn from Responses

**Goal**: Use response data to iterate on your approach and targeting.

After reaching out to all 30:

- **Count responses**: How many replied? (Target: at least 1-5 for validation)
- **If 0 responses**:
  - The message isn't compelling enough, OR
  - You're not solving a felt problem, OR
  - You're targeting the wrong people
- **If 1-5 responses**:
  - Good signal - analyze what these respondents have in common
  - These are your real ICP signals
- **If 5+ responses**:
  - Strong signal - you're onto something
  - Double down on this segment

**Decision Tree**:
- 0 responses → Change messaging OR change target audience
- 1-3 responses → Run another experiment with 30 more (similar profile)
- 5+ responses → You've found a pattern - expand to more of this profile

### Step 5: Conduct Learning Calls (Not Sales Calls)

**Goal**: Use first calls to learn, validate, and build relationships - not to sell.

**Mindset**: "I'm an early stage startup with a lot to learn. Can I gain your insight?"

**Opening the Call** (First 5 minutes):
```
"Thank you for taking the time. I want to be upfront - we're an early stage startup.
We're deeply passionate about solving [problem]. We have a lot to learn.

Here's how we're thinking about [problem] - does this resonate with how you're
experiencing it?"
```

**Be Vulnerable**:
- Tell them you're early stage (even if you have a product)
- Say there's "still a lot to be done"
- This makes them give honest feedback vs. polite feedback

**Key Questions to Ask**:
1. "How is this problem manifesting on your side?"
2. "Are you measuring or managing this problem today?" (If no, move on)
3. "How have you tried to solve it?" (Look for budget allocation, headcount, tools)
4. "Walk me through what's in your head right now" (Let them visualize the solution)

**Signs You're Onto Something**:
- The problem is growing/widening (not stable or shrinking)
- They're already measuring or managing it
- They've tried to solve it (hired someone, bought a tool, built something)
- They start pulling in colleagues: "I need to bring my boss/team into this"

**Red Flags**:
- They don't know if they measure/manage the problem
- They haven't tried to solve it at all
- The problem is "nice to have" not urgent
- They're being polite but not engaged

### Step 6: End Every Call with Next Steps

**Goal**: Book the next meeting before getting off the call.

- Pull up your calendar on the call
- Ask: "Who else should be invited to the next conversation?"
- Schedule the second call right there
- If they say "I'll email you later" → This is usually a soft no

**Follow-up Strategy**:
- Send a brief recap email within 24 hours
- Mention 2-3 key insights you learned
- Confirm the next meeting time
- Ask if there's anything to prepare

### Step 7: Iterate and Expand

**Goal**: Use learnings to refine your approach and scale to the next 10.

After your first 10 calls:

- **Pattern Recognition**:
  - What do the 3 most engaged prospects have in common?
  - What objections came up repeatedly?
  - What language/terminology do they use?

- **Message Refinement**:
  - Update your outreach template based on what resonated
  - Incorporate their language into your messaging
  - Sharpen your counterintuitive hook

- **Targeting Refinement**:
  - Narrow or expand your ICP based on response patterns
  - Look for adjacent segments with similar characteristics
  - Validate your pricing assumptions (can they afford what you're thinking?)

- **Scale Thoughtfully**:
  - Now reach out to the next 30-50 using refined approach
  - Still stay manual until you have 7-10 customers
  - Build conviction before automating

## Expected Outcomes

After completing this framework:

- **3-10 scheduled calls** from your first 30 prospects
- **1-3 paying customers** (could be pilot/design partner arrangements)
- **Clear pattern recognition** about who your best prospects are
- **Refined messaging** that consistently gets responses
- **Deep qualitative insights** about the problem you're solving
- **Conviction about next steps** (pivot, persevere, or expand)

**Important**: Revenue is NOT the primary goal at this stage. Learning velocity is.

## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Using Automation Tools Too Early
**Problem**: Founders jump to Clay, Apollo, or other tools before understanding their ICP.

**Why It Fails**:
- You blast 1,000 people with generic messages
- Your domain health gets damaged
- You don't learn anything from low engagement
- You waste the one chance to make a first impression

**Solution**: Stay manual until you have 7-10 customers and clear patterns.

### Pitfall 2: Focusing on Solution Instead of Problem
**Problem**: First emails talk about "our AI-powered platform that..."

**Why It Fails**:
- Everyone is pitching solutions
- Buyers are fatigued by product pitches
- You haven't earned the right to sell yet

**Solution**: Talk about the problem and your unique perspective on it. Make them say "tell me more."

### Pitfall 3: Mistaking Politeness for Interest
**Problem**: Prospects say "this is great!" but never move forward.

**Why It Fails**:
- People are nice and don't want to hurt founders' feelings
- Positive words don't equal buying intent
- You waste time on false positives

**Solution**:
- Look for actions, not words
- Do they bring in colleagues?
- Do they book the next meeting immediately?
- Are they asking detailed questions?
- If they won't schedule a follow-up on the call → it's a no

### Pitfall 4: Asking Generic Discovery Questions
**Problem**: "What keeps you up at night?" "If you had a magic wand..." "What are your pain points?"

**Why It Fails**:
- These answers change daily
- Too broad to be actionable
- Everyone asks these questions

**Solution**: Ask specific questions about measurement, management, and prior solution attempts.

### Pitfall 5: Trying to Boil the Ocean
**Problem**: Targeting "anyone in marketing at tech companies"

**Why It Fails**:
- Too broad to find commonalities
- Messaging becomes generic
- You can't build conviction about a segment

**Solution**: Start narrow (even absurdly narrow). You can expand later. Better to own a small segment than get ignored by a large one.

### Pitfall 6: Discounting Too Early
**Problem**: Offering 50% off to get the first customer signed.

**Why It Fails**:
- Sets bad precedent for pricing
- If they're not willing to pay reasonable price, they're not a good customer
- Best customers don't nickel-and-dime you
- You create pricing anchors that haunt you later

**Solution**:
- Price confidently (even if you offer pilot/design partner terms)
- If they're price-sensitive at $10K, they won't buy at $5K either - it's not about price
- Use service-based pricing if needed (easier to sell, builds trust)

## Key Metrics to Track

- **Outreach Conversion Rate**: Responses / Messages Sent (Target: 5-15% for founder-led)
- **Call Booking Rate**: Calls Scheduled / Responses (Target: 50%+)
- **Win Rate**: Customers Signed / Calls Completed (Target: 15-30%)
- **Learning Velocity**: New insights per call (Qualitative)
- **Time to Response**: How quickly do they reply? (Faster = more urgent problem)

**Important**: At this stage, conversion rate is less important than win rate. A 5% conversion rate with 30% win rate beats 15% conversion with 5% win rate.

## AI Integration Notes

**Context Signals for AI Models**:
- User mentions "getting first customers", "no customers yet", "pre-revenue"
- User asks about "customer acquisition" at very early stage
- User mentions ARR < $500K or "pre-seed/seed stage"
- User expresses uncertainty about target market or ICP

**Adaptation Guidance**:
- For B2B SaaS: Emphasize LinkedIn and email outreach
- For B2C: Adapt to community-based outreach (Reddit, Discord, etc.)
- For Enterprise: Cold calling becomes even more important
- For SMB: Higher volume needed, but same principles apply

**Limitations**:
- This framework is for founder-led sales only (pre-$1M ARR typically)
- Does not apply to product-led growth (PLG) models
- Assumes the founder has time to dedicate to sales (not always true)
- Works best for B2B; B2C requires different approaches
- Cold outreach regulations vary by region (GDPR, CAN-SPAM, etc.)

**Follow-up Recommendations**:
- If user reports low response rates → Suggest message refinement or targeting shift
- If user reports high interest but no conversions → Explore qualification and pricing issues
- If user has 5+ customers → Recommend transitioning to sales motion discovery
- If user is at $1M ARR → Recommend hiring first salesperson
